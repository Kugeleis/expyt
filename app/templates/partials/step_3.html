Step 3: Statistical Method Selection

Select an applicable statistical method evaluated against the properties of your filtered data.

ℹ️ Why are some methods not listed or disabled?
Statistical methods are verified against the properties of your filtered data:
  • Independent Two-Sample t-test: Requires exactly 2 groups of numeric data, with n ≥ 2 per group, and normality satisfied for all groups.
  • One-way ANOVA: Requires ≥ 2 groups of numeric data, with n ≥ 2 per group, normality satisfied, and homogeneous variance.
  • Mann-Whitney U: Non-parametric alternative to t-test; requires exactly 2 groups of numeric data with n ≥ 2 per group.
  • Kruskal-Wallis H: Non-parametric alternative to ANOVA; requires ≥ 2 groups of numeric data with n ≥ 2 per group.
  • Chi-Square test: Requires discrete/categorical outcome data across ≥ 2 groups.
  • Hierarchical Regression (GMM): Requires hierarchical configuration enabled.
